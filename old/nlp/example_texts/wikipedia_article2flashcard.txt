A somatostatinoma is a {{c1::tumor originating from the pancreatic delta cells}}.

These are classified as {{c2::pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors}} and are often associated with psammoma bodies.

The clinical presentation of a somatostatinoma may include:

{{c3::achlorhydria}}, resulting from the inhibition of gastrin secretion,

{{c4::gallstones and steatorrhea}}, due to the inhibition of secretin and cholecystokinin (CCK),

{{c5::diabetes}}, as a consequence of insulin inhibition.
